Caged: An Analysis of Incarceration in Furi I will be examining the themes related to incarceration, mental health and freedom in the game Furi (2016) by developer The Game Bakers. I will apply frameworks provided by Martin Buber, Jean Paul Sartre, and Mugambi Jouet, of the philosphy of dialogue, existentionalism and the prison industrial complex, respectively. Through these perspectives I will explore how the game developers implemented said concepts into the narrative and gameplay of the action game Furi. :

Narrative- You play as "The Stranger", a man who has been imprisoned for an indetermined amount of time, and for an unknown cause. The game begins with first person perspective scene where a jailer in a 3 headed mask vows to infinitely punish you, stating " I will kill you again and again. I am your future: an eternity of slow, painful deaths". Waking from your unconscious, you hear a disembodied voice urging you to "Pull yourself together. Get out of prison. Kill the jailer. Fight for your freedom. The jailer is the key, Kill him and you'll be free".

Psyche- In this scene we can assume that the rabbit character is likely a manifestation of the Stranger's subconscious as it refers to "We" while the Stranger does not speak or respond.Taking a closer look at the character reveals that it's movements in the games cutscenes are unnatural, it travels via teleportation and hovers over surfaces and repeatedly appears in locations inaccessible to the player. The Voice wields a microphone stand yet seems to speak only to the Stranger and directly to the player, he also does not appear on screen with any other character simultaneously. I believe this character represents the broken psyche of the Stranger as a result of incarceration, this manifestation works to justify the Stranger's motivationto murder his way out of prison. I could not help but draw parallels to other fictional rabbit figures in the films like "Harvey the Rabbit" (1950, Dir. Henry Koster) and Frank "Donnie Darko (2000, Dir. Richard Kelly) that guided the protagonists through their narratives. I find it interesing that the progression of these rabbit characters has lead to more anthropomorphic traits; Harvey the rabbit did not speak, Frank the Rabbitprimarily disguised himself in a rabbit suit and finally the Voice only wears an oversized partial mask. I believe the juxtaposition between a comically large rabbit head only partially obscuring the human within,represents the duality of the "good/bad" internal voice used in media. The rabbit representing innoncence and while the human within urges the player to kill.


Another character that speaks to the relationship between mental health and incarceration is the second boss fight known as "The Strap". As we approach a sphericla prsion, the player learns from the Voice that the Strap lives in a "Clever prison within a prison". The Strap character first appears bound with arms behind her back, feet fixed to a motorized wheel and a metal contraption completely encasing her head. Though she is capable of freeing her arms and legs as the battle progresses, she is never able to free her head. I believe this character is supposed to be a stand in for the practice of solitary confinementand the stigmatization of mental illness within the prison system The connections between this character's straight jacket design and mental illness becomes clear as the Voice tells the player " She was so wild they didn't know what to do with her, so they put her in a cage. They built up the walls to keep her in and so you couldn't hear her screaming" (Include Audio of The Strap's shrieking)
